M: TO THE READER:
M: This is a tale of witchcraft, told as a simple family of
M: seventeenth century New England might have believed it to be. All
M: of their folkloric and religious beliefs, in this film, are true.
M: It was inspired by various folktales, fairytales and recorded
M: accounts (journals, diaries, court records etc.) of historical
M: witches and possessions from New England and Western Europe before
M: the Salem outbreak in 1692. Much of the dialogue, in fact, comes
M: directly from those sources.
M: In order to effectively depict this world in which ordinary people
M: understood supernatural occurrences to be an expected part of
M: life, it is essential that all aspects of the film to be carried
M: out with utter naturalism. The characters must appear as real
M: farmers, not actors with dirty faces. Even the supernatural
M: elements must be photographed as realistically as possible. Yet,
M: with all this authenticity and “realism," it is still a folktale,
M: a dream. A nightmare from the past.
