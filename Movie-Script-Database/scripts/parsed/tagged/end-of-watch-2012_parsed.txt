M: “END OF WATCH” DIRECTOR’S STATEMENT
M: IMPORTANT PLEASE READ
M: To the reader from the writer and director:
M: I would like to help you understand my vision for “End of Watch.” In the simplest terms, this is a
M: film about two best friends who happen to be policemen. We follow their lives over a period of
M: several months as they fall in love, have children, get married and otherwise live their lives. The
M: film also has a strong crime story and plenty of action of great scope. It is a film that will have
M: something for everyone and touch upon universal themes of friendship, love, honor and courage.
M: These are real people leading real lives.
M: Today’s young people create and consume video media in very different ways. They film their daily
M: lives and share these videos through social media. It is this novel use of video and the intimacy of
M: reality that I wish to emulate. This film will be shot in many of the same ways that young people
M: create media today. My concept is simple: The characters themselves are placing and operating the
M: cameras. This creates a very visceral reality, much like watching real dramatic action on the
M: Internet. One could term this method “French New Wave Cinema meets You Tube.”
M: Often “found footage” films are difficult to watch due to excessive camera movement. Please keep
M: in mind that these will not be shaky, unsteady “Cloverfield” type images. “End of Watch” will be shot
M: in a normal theatrical resolution using many standard production techniques. Image quality will be
M: high, the colors will be vibrant and beautiful. The shots will be incredibly lyrical and smooth.
M: Although the actors will be wearing cameras, the images produced will be much like typical
M: Steadicam shots, with the same grace and beauty, although from angles before unachievable.
M: I am working with camera manufacturers to develop new cameras that will capture the images of
M: theatrical level quality yet be very small and compact. The extremely small, yet full resolution
M: cameras will be able to record the drama and action in ways that have not been possible before. In
M: success these new cameras will be nothing short of revolutionary. I believe the techniques used
M: will be groundbreaking unto themselves and will become an important talking point in the release
M: of the film. Of course there will also be standard camera coverage; my intent is to use all tools
M: available to tell this wonderful story. This film will be beautiful, clearly shot and will immerse the
M: viewer in it’s world in an intimate way.
M: “End of Watch” will deliver an exciting and fresh viewing experience for audiences of all
M: backgrounds. There will be nuanced and powerful performances by some of the best actors
M: working today. The audience will see police as they have never seen them, the journey will be
M: vibrant, fun and exciting. It will have the entertainment value o f a “popcorn movie” yet deliver the
M: depth and sophistication of a powerful drama. I hope you enjoy the script and come on this
M: filmmaking journey with me.
M: Kind regards,
M: David Ayer
M: Los Angeles, California
